Fission

Fission is a form of radioactive decay, or decay of an atom.
Rather than most radioactive decay which happens over a long time. Fission can happen in seconds.

Fission occurs when a neutron is thrown at an unstable isotope of an atom, or the number of neutrons, such as helium 13;
Or Uranium - 235.

Fission splits the atom in half, creating 2 new identical elements, and releasing a huge amount of energy in the process.

Fission can happen anywhere, even inside stars. Inside stars they are very much less of the mass of the star, but some heavy elements are made. Some even radioactive!

Alpha Radioactive Decay

Alpha Radioactive Decay, or just Alpha Decay occurs over time to Radioactive elements/Unstable Elements.

Alpha Decay occurs over a short period of time rather than seconds with fission.

Alpha Decay releases alpha particles, which contain 2 proton, and 2 neutrons from the original atom, which changes the element and the isotope as well.

Beta Radioactive Decay

Unlike Alpha Decay and Fission, Beta Radioactive Decay takes thousands of years to occur.
Beta Decay over thousands of years releases an electron and a antineutrino, making the neutron into a proton, changing the element and changing the charge of the atom a little bit.
Depending on the electron amount in the atom, this could make it into an positively charged ion, or make it neutrally charged, or even make it a negatively charged ion.
